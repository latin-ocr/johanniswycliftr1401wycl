general of all, since all may be resolved into it; it is in the form of
a conditional syllogism, in which the condition is inferred from
the conditioned proposition. IfPompey killed Caesar, Caesar is
dead; but Caesar is dead; . · . Pompey killed Caesar. In this case
it would be: If(A is B and C is D), then A is B,· hut A is B . ·.
